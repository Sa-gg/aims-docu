> **Warning:** Colegado (2025) is a REAL paper with a real DOI, but it is **misrepresented** in v3.  
> - **Actual title:** "Digital Innovations in Science Education in the Philippines: A Scoping Review of Teaching Practices and Tools" (*IJRISS, Vol. 9, No. 3S*, pp. 6549–6556)  
> - **What the paper actually says:** A scoping review of 37 Philippine K-12 science studies; finds that LMS platforms (Google Classroom, Quipper) were used for content delivery and assessment monitoring but mainly in urban or well-resourced schools; highlights infrastructure and teacher preparation barriers.  
> - **What v3 cited it as saying:** "educators recognized the transformative potential of digital assessment tools, [but] a critical limitation was the absence of integrated automated evaluation features aligned with local curriculum requirements" — **this specific claim is NOT in the paper.**  
> - **Action needed:** In the next version, either (a) re-cite Colegado with what the paper actually states, or (b) find a different local source that specifically discusses automated grading limitations in Philippine schools.
